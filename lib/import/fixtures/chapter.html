The Fifth Bell

The fifth bell rang over the harbor just as Serin reached the western bridge, and every lantern along the rails answered with a pulse of amber light.

Below, the tide moved like black glass. Boats drifted in silence, their hulls wrapped in fog so thick the city seemed to float on cloud instead of water.

She kept one hand on the wrapped key at her belt and counted the towers ahead, remembering the old map hidden behind the nursery wall.

When the sixth step cracked beneath her boot, she froze. A second later, a voice rose from under the bridge and spoke her childhood name with terrible calm.

Serin did not look down. She stared at the palace lights instead and realized they were arranged in the pattern of the royal seal, a warning visible only from the water.

The bell sounded again, deeper this time, and the sound rolled through the iron ribs of the bridge until it felt as if the whole city had become a tuning fork.

By the time she reached the gatehouse, the guards were already waiting, their spears lowered and their eyes fixed on the key she had failed to keep hidden.

She smiled anyway. If the city had finally chosen to recognize her, it would have to hear what she had come back to say.
